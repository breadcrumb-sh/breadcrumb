The Traces page lets you spot patterns across your traces, catch issues with AI-generated findings, and drill into individual requests to see exactly what happened.

## Overview

![Traces overview showing common agent paths, span frequency per trace, and filtering options](/docs/traces.webp)

Filter by date range, trace name, span type, and status.

**Common agent paths** - shows the most frequent sequences of spans across your traces. See which paths your agents take and how often.

**Span frequency per trace** - bar chart showing how many times each span appears per trace at p5, p50, and p95. Useful for spotting loops or unexpected repetition.

**Span loopback rate** - tracks how often spans repeat within a single trace. High loopback rates usually mean an agent is retrying or stuck.

## Individual traces

![Individual trace view with span tree, timeline, and detail panel showing prompts and completions](/docs/trace.webp)

Click any trace to see the full span tree. Each span shows:

- **Timeline** - visual bar showing when each span started and how long it ran
- **Span tree** - nested view of all spans with type badges, duration, and token counts
- **Detail panel** - click a span to see its full input (system/user messages), output, model, provider, and custom metadata

Every span captures the actual prompts sent and responses received - not summaries, not samples.

Use **Clean view** to collapse the span tree into a flat, readable conversation flow - just the inputs and outputs in order, without the nesting.
